    Worksheet 🏫 Activity 7 - Laplace Transforms
    

    Instructions: Write solutions to each of the following problems in the space provided. You are encouraged to work in groups, but also encouraged to use your own words to express your ideas. This activity is due at the beginning of class on Monday, July 27.
    🔗

    1.

    The Dirac Delta function is a very contoversial tool used (out of necessity) in many physical sciences, depsite the fact it is not mathematically well-defined. Its "definition" is
    \begin{equation*} \delta(t - t_0) = \begin{cases} \infty & \ \text{if} t = t_0; \\ 0 & \ \text{otherwise}. \end{cases} \end{equation*}
    and is "forced" to have the property
    \begin{equation*} \int_{-\infty}^\infty \delta(t-t_0) \, dt = 1. \end{equation*}
    🔗

    (a)

    Explain how exhibiting both of the properties in its definition might be controversial.
    🔗
    🔗

    (b)

    Show that, for \(t_0 \gt 0 \) we have
    \begin{equation*} \mathcal{L} \{\delta(t-t_0) \} = e^{-st_0}. \end{equation*}
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    2.

    Use Laplace transforms to solve the BVP
    \begin{equation*} y'' + 8y' + 20y = 0; \hspace{10pt} y(0) = y'(\pi) = 0 . \end{equation*}
    🔗
    🔗

    3.

    The voltage across a particular circuit is described by the differential equation
    \begin{equation*} u'' + 4u' + 5u = E(t) \end{equation*}
    where \(E(t)\) is the impressed voltage.
    🔗

    (a)

    An engineer sends an instantaneous 3V pulse into the circuit after 2 seconds. Assuming the circuit starts at rest, write the corresponding IVP.
    🔗
    🔗

    (b)

    Use Laplace transforms to find a solution to the IVP you wrote in part (a).
    🔗
    🔗

    (c)

    Explain the qualitative behavior of the system. In particular, describe what is happening to the current in the circuit before, during, and after the pulse is applied.
