Worksheet Exercise 3.

Any differential equation of the form
\begin{equation} \dydx + P(x)y = Q(x) y^n\tag{4} \end{equation}
where \(n\) is called a Bernoulli equation.
🔗

(a)

Show that, when \(n=0\text{,}\) the Bernoulli equation is linear.
🔗
🔗

(b)

Show that, when \(n=1\text{,}\) the Bernoulli equation is linear.
🔗
🔗

(c)

Suppose \(n\neq 0\) and \(n \neq 1\text{.}\) First, divide both sides of (4) by \(y^n\text{.}\) Then use the substitution \(u = y^{n-1}\) to reduce the Bernoulli equation to be linear.
