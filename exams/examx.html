There are 110 points, but this exam will be graded out of 100 points, meaning there are 10 points of extra credit.

    1.

    [12 points] Consider the differential equation
    \begin{equation*} \frac{d^2y}{dx^2} - 2x \frac{dy}{dx} + 2y = 0. \end{equation*}
    🔗

    (a)

    Determine the order of the equation above. Justify your answer.
    🔗
    🔗

    (b)

    Is the equation ordinary or partial? How do you know?
    🔗
    🔗

    (c)

    Is the equation linear or nonlinear? How do you know?
    🔗
    🔗

    (d)

    Is this equation homogeneous? How do you know?
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    2.

    [16 points] Use separation of variables to find a solution to the initial-value problem
    \begin{equation*} \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{2x+1}{2y}; \hspace{10pt} y(-2)=-1. \end{equation*}
    🔗
    🔗

    3.

    [16 points] Solve the differential equation
    \begin{equation*} 4y \, dx = 4(y-x) \, dy \end{equation*}
    using any method
    🔗
    🔗

    4.

    [20 points] Solve the differential equation
    \begin{equation*} (x + ye^{y/x}) \, dx - x e^{y/x} \, dy = 0. \end{equation*}
    using the substitution \(u = y/x \text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗

    5.

    [24 points] Newton’s Law of Cooling says that the rate at which a body cools is proportional to the difference in its temperature \(T\) and the ambient temperature \(T_m\text{.}\)
    🔗

    (a)

    Write a differential equation that models Newton’s Law of Cooling.
    🔗
    🔗

    (b)

    Find a general solution to the model you wrote in part (a).
    🔗
    🔗

    (c)

    Suppose a thermometer is taken out of a \(70^\circ\)F room and placed outside where the ambient temperature is \(10^\circ\)F. After 30 seconds, the thermometer has fallen to \(50^\circ\)F. Use your answer from part (b) to write a model for the temperature of the thermometer \(T\) after \(t\) seconds.
    🔗
    🔗

    (d)

    Determine how long will it take the thermometer to reach 15\(^\circ\)F.
    🔗
    🔗

    (e)

    In your own words, explain the difference between the model you gave in part (a) and part (c).
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    7.

    [13 points] Consider the IVP
    \begin{equation*} \frac{dy}{dx} = y^2 - 3y. \end{equation*}
    🔗

    (a)

    Identify the slope function of this differential equation.
    🔗
    🔗

    (b)

    Find all equilibria (nullclines) for the system.
    🔗
    🔗

    (c)

    Use your answer(s) for part (b) to plot a phase portrait of the system on the set of axes below. Clearly sketch and label all equilibria and identify regions of increasing/descreasing slope.
    🔗

    (d)

    Classify the equilibria you found in part (b) as stable, unstable, or neither. Use the phase portrait your produced in part (c) to justify your answer.
